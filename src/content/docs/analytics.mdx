Identity is a goldmine of security and product signal. Keycloak emits rich events; we capture,
store, and surface them — the analytics layer is a first-class part of the platform, not a
bolt-on.

## The pipeline

```
Keycloak (Event Listener SPI) ──▶ durable queue ──▶ ClickHouse ──▶ dashboards / exports / metering
```

- **Event Listener SPI** — Keycloak's native extension point emits **user events** (login,
  logout, register, MFA, failures, token refresh) and **admin events** (config changes) with
  no polling.
- **Durable queue** — decouples Keycloak from analytics so a spike or outage never backs up
  the auth hot path.
- **ClickHouse** — a columnar store built for high-cardinality event analytics at scale.

<Aside type="note">
This is the same first-party, cookieless approach we use for our own site analytics: capture
events at the source, enrich server-side, aggregate on a schedule. Your tenant's data stays in
your tenant.
</Aside>

## Usage metering & billing

The same event stream powers **metering**. We count **Monthly Active Users** — a unique user
who authenticates at least once in a calendar month; token refreshes and introspections don't
add MAUs — and feed it to billing (Stripe).

<Aside type="tip" title="Billing unit">
MAU is the industry-standard, predictable unit (same as Auth0/Cognito/Okta), and our low,
mostly-fixed cost base means engagement swings don't threaten your bill. Machine-to-machine
(client-credentials) token traffic is metered separately, since it doesn't map to a human user.
</Aside>

## Retention & privacy

- Event retention is configurable per plan; raw events age out on a lifecycle policy.
- Analytics run on **your tenant's** data; no cross-tenant mixing.
- IP handling follows your compliance posture (full, anonymized, or geo-only).
